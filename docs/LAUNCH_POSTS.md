## Dev.to / Hashnode Article

### Title: Why I Built Et3am: An Open-Source Food Donation Platform for the Arab World

---

**Three years ago**, I watched a hotel buffet being thrown away while driving past a neighborhood where I knew families struggled to afford their next meal. That visual stuck with me.

In Egypt alone, we waste ~35% of the food we produce. Simultaneously, **1 in 3 Egyptians** faces some level of food insecurity. This isn't a supply problem — it's a logistics and connection problem.

I wanted to build something that could help, something that:
- Doesn't take a cut from donations
- Works in Arabic first (because that's what my community needs)
- Is open source so anyone can verify, contribute, or fork it
- Actually gets food to people who need it, fast

**Et3am (إطعام)** is the result.

---

**The technical journey:**

I started with React + TypeScript for the frontend — wanted type safety and a component system that scales. Express + PostgreSQL for the backend — needed something familiar and reliable. Socket.io for real-time updates — because when someone reserves a donation, both parties should know immediately.

The trickiest part was making maps work well in Arabic. Leaflet + right-to-left support + Arabic labels took some iteration, but it works now.

---

**Why open source?**

Because food security isn't a problem one company can solve. It needs community involvement. Restaurants, hotels, households, volunteers, NGOs — everyone has a role. Making Et3am open means:

- Transparency: Anyone can verify where their donations go
- Localization: Contributors can adapt it for their countries
- Sustainability: The community owns the tool, not a single company

---

**Want to help?**

We're just getting started. Check our GitHub for good first issues. Whether you're a developer, translator, or just want to spread the word — there's a way to contribute.

Let's make sure food reaches people, not landfills.
